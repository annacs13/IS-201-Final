St. Louis Blues

What are the Blues?

The St. Louis Blues are a professional ice hockey team based in St. Louis, Missouri, playing in the NHL's Western Conference.

Founded in 1967 as part of the league's expansion, the team was named after the genre of music associated with the city, reflecting St. Louis's rich musical heritage.

The Blues play at the Enterprise Center, previously known as the Scottrade Center, located in downtown St. Louis. The team's colors of blue, gold, and white are a nod to their musical roots, and their beloved mascot, Louie the Blue, is a favorite among fans. Known for their loyal fanbase and thrilling playoff runs, the St. Louis Blues remain a significant force in American hockey.

2019: The Magical Year

The 2018-2019 St. Louis Blues season was a remarkable journey, culminating in the franchise's first-ever Stanley Cup championship. The team had a rocky start, sitting last in the NHL standings as late as January 3, 2019. However, a midseason turnaround sparked by the arrival of head coach Craig Berube and the stellar play of goaltender Jordan Binnington changed the course of their season. With a mix of veteran leadership and young talent, the Blues went on an incredible run, making the playoffs as the third seed in the Central Division. In the postseason, they defeated the Winnipeg Jets, Dallas Stars, and San Jose Sharks, with several hard-fought series victories. In the Stanley Cup Final, St. Louis faced the Boston Bruins, a team heavily favored to win. The Blues showed resilience and determination, ultimately winning the series 4-3. The Cup-clinching Game 7 victory, a 4-1 win in Boston, was sealed by goals from Brayden Schenn, Alex Pietrangelo, and an empty-netter by Carl Gunnarsson. The championship was a testament to the Blues' perseverance, and their win resonated deeply with the city, as it was the culmination of nearly five decades of heartache and near-misses.
